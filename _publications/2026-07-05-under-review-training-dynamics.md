Recent studies on feature reliance in convolutional neural networks investigate how models rely on visual features such as shape, texture, and color under controlled evaluation protocols. This work systematically studies whether such analyses remain robust to training variability across random seeds, learning rates, weight decay, and training budgets. The study uses a fully automated and reproducible pipeline across Caltech101, Flowers102, STL10, and Oxford-IIIT Pet with ResNet50, showing that training dynamics can introduce substantial variability and that robust interpretability evaluation requires carefully controlled training pipelines.
